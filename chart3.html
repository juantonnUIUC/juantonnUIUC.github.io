Average Cable Viewership per Game vs. Average Salary in American Professional Sports Leagues

This visualization compares the average per game viewership on cable TV against the average salary in prominent American professional sports leagues. This interactive visualization allows you to explore the relationship between fan engagement and player compensation in various leagues, including the Women's National Basketball Association (WNBA), National Basketball Association (NBA), Major League Soccer (MLS), National Hockey League (NHL), Major League Baseball (MLB), and National Football League (NFL).

Chart Overview:

The scatter plot juxtaposes the "Average Cable Viewership per Game" and "Average Salary" metrics for each league. By default, the WNBA data point is displayed, representing the league's starting point for comparison. Users can then choose to display additional leagues by selecting them from checkboxes provided.

Purpose:

This visualization aims to highlight the correlation between viewership numbers and player salaries across different professional sports leagues.

How to Use:

  1. Toggle League Selection: Use the checkboxes to select one or more leagues you wish to compare on the scatter plot. By default, the WNBA point is shown for initial comparison.
  2. Analyze the Data: Once you've selected your preferred leagues, the scatter plot will display the respective data points, each representing a league. Hover over the data points to view specific league information, including average viewership and average salary.
